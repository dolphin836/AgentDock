# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

AgentDock is for macOS developers who work with Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, or several AI agents at once. They are usually focused inside a terminal or editor and need an ambient way to see which agents are active, which one needs attention, and how much usage remains without opening another dashboard.

## Product Purpose

AgentDock brings live local AI-agent status, approvals, usage, and workspace return actions into the macOS notch or menu bar. The website should help a qualified visitor understand the product in ten seconds, trust how it handles local data and permissions, and confidently download the current Mac build.

## Brand Personality

Precise, quiet, trustworthy. AgentDock should feel like a thoughtful native macOS utility: technically capable, calm under load, and respectful of the developer's attention.

## Anti-references

- Generic neon AI landing pages with glowing gradients and vague intelligence imagery.
- Decorative glassmorphism and translucent cards used without a functional reason.
- Full-page terminal cosplay that makes ordinary marketing copy harder to read.
- Repetitive SaaS feature-card grids and oversized rounded icon tiles.
- Unrelated promotions or public admin links that dilute product trust.

## Design Principles

1. Show the notch experience instead of merely describing it.
2. Make the product's interruption-saving value understandable within ten seconds.
3. Use real product states, approvals, and usage data as the visual language.
4. Explain local data handling, permissions, and telemetry with concrete language.
5. Keep every page element focused on understanding, trust, or downloading.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast and interaction. Support keyboard navigation and reduced motion. Never rely on color alone to communicate agent state. Keep essential text readable at mobile sizes and preserve clear hierarchy in both English and Simplified Chinese.
